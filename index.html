How to Make Masala tea

Masala tea is a flavorful and aromatic Indian tea made by brewing black tea with a mixture of spices, milk, and sugar. Here's a simple recipe to make masala tea at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup milk (whole or any preferred type)
  • 2 teaspoons black tea leaves (or 2 tea bags)
  • 1-2 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, grated
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Optional: a pinch of black pepper or fennel seeds for extra flavor

Staps:

  1. Boil Water and Spices: In a saucepan, bring the water to a boil. Add the grated ginger, crushed cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and any optional spices you like. Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes to allow the spices to infuse the water.
  2. Add Tea Leaves: Add the black tea leaves (or tea bags) to the boiling water and let it steep for 2-3 minutes. Adjust the steeping time based on how strong you want your tea.
  3. Add Milk and Sugar: Pour in the milk and add sugar to taste. Stir well and bring the mixture back to a gentle boil. Let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. Strain and Serve: Remove the saucepan from heat and strain the tea into cups using a fine mesh strainer to remove the tea leaves and spices. Serve hot.

Notes:

  • Feel free to adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences.
  • You can also experiment with different types of tea, such as green tea or herbal tea, for variations of this recipe.

Enjoy your homemade masala tea!
